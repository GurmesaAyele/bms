How to Prepare for Donation

Follow these simple steps to ensure a successful donation experience

24 Hours Before

  • Get a good night's sleep (7-8 hours)
  • Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, red meat, beans)
  • Stay well hydrated
  • Avoid alcohol

Day of Donation

  • Eat a healthy breakfast or lunch
  • Drink extra fluids (16 oz of water)
  • Bring valid photo ID
  • Wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that roll up
  • Bring a list of medications you're taking

After Donation

  • Rest for 10-15 minutes
  • Drink plenty of fluids for the next 24 hours
  • Avoid heavy lifting or vigorous exercise for 24 hours
  • Keep the bandage on for 4-5 hours
  • Eat iron-rich foods for the next few days

Eat Well

Have a good meal before donating. Avoid fatty foods as they can affect blood tests.

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water before and after donation to help your body recover.

Get Rest

A good night's sleep helps ensure you're in the best condition to donate.

Know Your Medications

Bring a list of all medications and supplements you're currently taking.

Common Myths About Blood Donation

Let's separate fact from fiction

Myth: It's Painful

Fact: You'll feel a brief pinch when the needle is inserted, but the donation itself is painless.

Myth: It Makes You Weak

Fact: Your body quickly replaces the donated blood. Most people feel fine immediately after.

Myth: You Can Get Sick

Fact: All equipment is sterile and used only once. There's no risk of infection from donating.

Myth: It Takes Too Long

Fact: The actual donation takes only 8-12 minutes. The entire process is about an hour.
